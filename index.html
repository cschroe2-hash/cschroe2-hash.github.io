Christopher A. Schroeder

I am a PhD student in the Department of Mathematics and Statistics at Binghamton University in Binghamton, New York, where I study finite groups and their representation theory with Hung Tong-Veit. Previously, I completed a PhD in physics at the University of Maryland under Luis Orozco. During my PhD studies, I was a guest at Martin Plenio's Institute for Theoretical Physics in Ulm, Germany, where I studied quantum effects in photosynthesis. Before that, I graduated from the University of Oklahoma with a BS in mathematics, a BS in physics and a BA in German. I am a committed educator with an interest in translation. For more information about me, please see my CV.
